| Forecasting taxi demand   | Online taxi-service plans to predict the number of taxi orders for the next hour to bring in more drivers during peak periods.| Pandas, scikit-learn, matplotlib, optuna, CatBoost, LightGBM, Prophet | Finished |

**Goal:** develop a machine learning model for predicting the number of orders for the next hour with the allocation of peak periods.

**Tasks:** analyze the given time series data, determine peak load periods and consider several models with hyperparameters optimization on the training set. Overall RMSE metrics should be less than 48 on the test set.

**Summary:** 
* Brief data analysis indicates, that the original dataset is characterized by 10 minute intervals. Considering that the task is to forecast the number of taxi orders for the next hour, data has been resampled in 1-hour intervals with the sum of orders for each hour. There are no missing values in the dataset and the dates are monotonous. The first observation was on March 1, 2018, and the last was on August 31 of the same year. The time delta is 183 days and 23 hours;
* Throughout the research horizon (March 2018-August 2018), there is a trend toward an increase in the volume of orders at airports. The decomposition of the series into components confirms the thesis of a growing trend. Taxi order volumes characterized by weekly and dayly seasonality. Thus, peaks occur at the end of the week (Friday) and the beginning (Monday), and order volumes fall from midnight to ~6 in the morning. From about 6 am until midnight, the growth of taxi orders begins with peaks around 9:00, 16:00, and midnight. The following features were created: order's hour and day of the week because the data contains daily and weekly seasonalities, lagging, and moving averages values;
* The best result was demonstrated by LGBM on the training set. RMSE on the training set is ~17.58, while MAE is ~13.28, i.e. the model is wrong by 13.28 orders on average. RMSE on the test set is ~36.69, MAE ~26.25. However, built models can be improved in the future. For example, it is possible to parse historical data on the number of arrivals/departures from airports with the type of aircraft (the goal is to determine an approximate number of passengers) and feed this number into the model as a feature.
